WG1: Smallest Scales – Vortex Dynamics and Dissipation

WG1 leaders: Daniel Pęcak, Giulia Del Pace

Scope

WG1 focuses on the smallest scales of superfluid dynamics, where the vortex structure, such as the vortex core, plays a key role and must be explicitly taken into account. At these scales, vortex dynamics and dissipation mechanisms depend sensitively on microscopic properties of the underlying superfluid.

From an experimental perspective, this includes detailed studies of the motion of a small number of vortices and their interactions with pinning sites. These processes can be investigated in ultracold atomic gases and by means of tracer particles in superfluid helium.

Approaches

From a theoretical perspective, microscopic techniques such as Quantum Monte Carlo methods will be employed to gain insight into vortex reconnections, dissipation mechanisms, and strongly interacting superfluids, including regimes relevant to neutron star interiors.

Vortex dynamics will also be investigated using numerical solutions of the Gross–Pitaevskii equation, allowing the study of different interaction strengths and geometries. In addition, density functional and hydrodynamical approaches will complement microscopic and mean-field methods, providing descriptions that can be extrapolated to larger scales and to astrophysical systems.

Key scientific question

How do vortex dynamics change across different fermionic and bosonic systems, and what is the role of the vortex core structure?

In addition, WG1 will address microscopic aspects of the Action-wide questions concerning the forces acting on vortices and their motion through inhomogeneous media.
