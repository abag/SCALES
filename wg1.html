WG1: SMALLEST SCALES - VORTEX DYNAMICS AND DISSIPATION

WG1 leaders: Daniel Pęcak, Giulia Del Pace

This WG deals with the smallest scales in the problem, on which the vortex structure, such as the vortex core, plays a role and must be taken into consideration when investigating superfluid dynamics. From the experimental side, this involves studying in detail the motion of a small number of vortices and their interactions with pinning sites. These processes can be readily examined in cold atoms experiments and with the use of tracer particles in helium.

From a theoretical perspective, the smallest scales will be investigated using microscopic techniques such as Quantum Monte Carlo methods, which provide insight into vortex reconnections in the presence of quantum turbulence and are particularly well suited to strongly interacting superfluids, including those relevant to neutron star interiors. Vortex dynamics will also be studied through numerical solutions of the Gross–Pitaevskii equation, while complementary approaches will include density functional and hydrodynamical methods, which can be extrapolated to neutron star interiors.

In particular, this WG will focus on the question:

How do vortex dynamics change across different (fermionic and bosonic) systems, and what is the role of the vortex core structure?

WG1 will also tackle microscopic aspects of the Action-wide questions on the forces acting on vortices and their motion through inhomogeneous media.
