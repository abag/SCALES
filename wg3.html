WG3: MACROSCOPIC SCALES - NEUTRON STARS AND THEIR LABORATORY ANALOGUES

WG3 leader: Nicholas Chamel

This WG deals with the largest scales of the problem at which the dynamics of the system is studied by mediating over a large number of vortices and applying hydrodynamical methods. This is the method of choice for neutron star studies, where general relativistic effects play a key role. Specific details of neutron star superfluidity remain, however, highly uncertain. As a result, theoretical and computational tools used for neutron star interiors must be benchmarked against laboratory systems in order to test the methods and constrain the parameters of cosmic superfluidity. It is thus fundamental to leverage laboratory condensates that are much simpler to analyse and provide an exciting testbed for studies of superfluid phenomena when constructing laboratory analogues of a rotating neutron star for exactly this purpose.

This WG will therefore focus mainly on the question:

How can laboratory results be used to constrain physics in neutron stars, and vice-versa? How can astronomical observations guide the choice of experimental setups?

and how to model the forces acting on a vortex on a hydrodynamical scale, specifically how the inhomogeneous environment of the neutron star crust affects the stellar dynamics?
