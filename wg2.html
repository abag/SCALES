WG2: INTERMEDIATE SCALES - SUPERFLUID TURBULENCE

WG2 leader: Emil Varga

WG2 focuses on the intermediate scales at which the dynamics of the vortex core can be ignored, but interactions between vortices become important. In this case, one can treat vortices as massless, and use VF methods to describe the results of experiments with a large number of vortices, in which superfluid turbulence may also develop. The latter is generally the case in experiments with helium-3 and helium-4 in rotating containers, but also in three-dimensional BEC systems and, possibly, in neutron star interiors following large-scale dynamical disruptions.

This WG will therefore focus mainly on the question:

How does turbulence emerge and decay in different superfluid systems?

and how turbulence is linked to the forces driving vortex motion and how it is affected by motion in inhomogeneous media?
